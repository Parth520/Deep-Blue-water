üåê Overview of the Indian Ocean

Covering around 27.24 million square miles (70.56 million square kilometers), the Indian Ocean lies nestled between Africa, Asia, Australia, and the Southern Ocean. Unlike the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans, it is largely enclosed by land, which gives it a unique character in terms of circulation and weather.

It is bounded by:

  • The east coast of Africa
  • The southern shores of Asia (India, Bangladesh, etc.)
  • The west coasts of Australia
  • The Southern Ocean to the south

The deepest point of the Indian Ocean is the Java Trench (also known as the Sunda Trench), which reaches a depth of about 7,258 meters (23,812 feet).


üìö General Knowledge and Key Features

The Indian Ocean is known for its warm waters, being the warmest of all the oceans. It plays a critical role in monsoon systems that affect the livelihoods of billions in South Asia, East Africa, and Southeast Asia.

Some defining characteristics of the Indian Ocean include:

  • Major Currents: Unlike the other oceans, the currents of the Indian Ocean change direction seasonally, mainly due to the monsoon winds.
  • Vital Trade Routes: It houses critical maritime trade routes that connect the Middle East, East Africa, India, and Southeast Asia to the rest of the world.

Important Islands and Archipelagos:

Key Seas and Gulfs:
