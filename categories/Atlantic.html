Spanning about 41 million square miles (106 million square kilometers), the Atlantic Ocean makes up roughly 20% of Earth‚Äôs surface. Its name derives from the ancient Greek "Atlantikos", meaning "Sea of Atlas."

The Atlantic is shaped like an elongated 'S', bordered by North and South America on the west and Europe and Africa on the east. It is divided into two parts: North Atlantic and South Atlantic.

üìö General Knowledge and Features

The Mid-Atlantic Ridge is a prominent underwater mountain range that runs down the center of the ocean, marking the divergent boundary between tectonic plates. It plays a critical role in seafloor spreading and volcanic activity.

The Atlantic is home to major ocean currents that influence global climate:

  • Gulf Stream: A powerful warm Atlantic current that affects the climate of North America and Western Europe.
  • North Atlantic Drift and Canary Current also shape regional weather patterns.

Major marginal seas and gulfs include the Caribbean Sea, Gulf of Mexico, Baltic Sea, North Sea, and Mediterranean Sea.

The Sargasso Sea, unique in being defined by ocean currents rather than land borders, floats in the North Atlantic and is known for its clear blue water and floating sargassum seaweed.
