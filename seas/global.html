üåä Caribbean Sea ‚Äì A Tropical Crossroads of Nature, History, and Mystery

The Caribbean Sea, a dazzling expanse of turquoise waters and sun-kissed islands, is one of the most iconic and biologically diverse seas in the world. Located in the western Atlantic Ocean, this sea has served as a central route for exploration, colonization, trade, and conflict for over five centuries. Its rich marine ecosystems, storied history, and vibrant cultures make it a unique body of water that continues to shape global tourism, science, and geopolitics.

üåê Overview of the Caribbean Sea

The Caribbean Sea is a tropical sea of the Atlantic Ocean, bordered by:

  • The Greater and Lesser Antilles to the north and east (including Cuba, Jamaica, Puerto Rico, and the Virgin Islands)
  • Central America (from Belize to Panama) to the west
  • Northern South America, including Venezuela and Colombia, to the south

It covers approximately 2.75 million square kilometers and reaches a maximum depth of about 7,686 meters (25,220 feet) in the Cayman Trench, making it one of the deepest seas in the world.

Climate and Weather: Located in the hurricane belt, prone to storms and hurricanes from June to November.

Tourism and Economy: Tourism, shipping, and fisheries dominate the economy. Cruise ships are a major contributor.

The Red Sea, with its striking blue waters framed by arid deserts and rugged mountains, stands as one of the most unique and historically significant seas in the world. Positioned between Africa and the Arabian Peninsula, the Red Sea is not just a natural wonder but a vital corridor for maritime trade, cultural exchange, and geological transformation.   • Bordered by Egypt, Sudan, and Eritrea to the west
  • Bordered by Saudi Arabia and Yemen to the east

Covering an area of about 438,000 square kilometers, the Red Sea is renowned for its extreme salinity, warm waters, and rich marine life.

Located in the North Atlantic Ocean, the Sargasso Sea is a floating marine forest of golden-brown algae, home to a fascinating ecosystem and vital to the life cycle of several marine species. üåê Overview of the Sargasso Sea

  • South: North Equatorial Current
  • West: Antilles Current

The Sargasso Sea is a natural anomaly‚Äîa sea without shores, sustained by circulating currents and floating forests. Protecting such a unique region is not just a scientific goal but a global responsibility.
